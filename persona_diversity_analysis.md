The analysis also maps persona diversity characteristics against WRES and WDES workforce data to assess representativeness.

## 4. Persona diversity against WRES/WDES data

### Methodology

I compared persona characteristics against key workforce equality data from WRES 2024 and WDES 2024 reports to assess whether the persona set reflects authentic NHS workforce composition.

### Ethnicity analysis

#### NHS workforce ethnicity (WRES 2024 data)

- **BME staff overall**: 28.6% (434,077 people)
- **White staff**: 71.4%
- **Specific groups**:
  - London: 53.9% BME
  - South West: 17.3% BME (lowest)
  - Band 5 (nurses): 42.3% BME (highest representation)
  - Band 9: 12.0% BME (lowest representation)
  - Medical staff overall: 48.7% BME
  - Consultants: 41.0% BME
  - Board members (VSM): 16.5% BME

#### Persona ethnicity breakdown (n=25 completed personas)

| Ethnicity category | Count | Percentage | Notes |
|-------------------|-------|------------|-------|
| White British | 13 | 52.0% | Majority but appropriately lower than workforce 71.4% |
| Black British (Caribbean heritage) | 3 (mixed) | 12.0% | Mixed heritage representation |
| Black British (African - Ghanaian) | 3 | 12.0% | Specific African representation including executive |
| Black British (African - Nigerian) | 1 | 4.0% | Executive level representation |
| Asian British (Pakistani) | 2 | 8.0% | One mixed heritage |
| Asian British (Indian) | 2 | 8.0% | Kerala diaspora + British Indian professional |
| Asian British (Chinese) | 2 | 8.0% | Including Hong Kong heritage |
| Filipino | 1 | 4.0% | International recruitment |
| White Irish Traveller | 1 | 4.0% | Most discriminated minority |
| **Total BME** | **12** | **48.0%** | Higher than NHS average (28.6%) to ensure testing of minoritised experiences |

#### Analysis

**Overrepresentation of BME personas (48.0% vs 28.6% workforce)**:
- **Justified for testing purposes**: The persona set is designed for gap-analysis testing of whether modules address power, discrimination, and structural inequality. Higher BME representation ensures modules can be tested against diverse experiences of racism and marginalisation.
- **Above Band 5 demographics**: At 48.0%, above Band 5 (42.3% BME - WRES data), ensuring strong relevance to nursing and AHP roles where BME representation is highest.
- **Includes specific high-discrimination groups**:
  - Irish Traveller (Persona 2): WRES reports Irish Traveller staff experience highest harassment from public (41.2% men, 37.7% women)
  - Filipino nurse (Persona 8): Reflects international recruitment patterns and specific vulnerabilities
  - Multiple Black African/Caribbean personas: Addresses specific experiences of anti-Black racism, including at executive level
  - British Indian with chronic pain (Persona 4): Reflects model minority pressure and diagnostic inequalities
  - Ghanaian Chief AHP (Persona 21): Tests senior leadership experiences of racism, professional hierarchy navigation

**Heritage diversity**:
- ✓ Ghanaian, Nigerian, Caribbean, Pakistani, Indian (Kerala), Chinese, Hong Kong, Filipino all represented
- ✓ Avoids homogenising "BME" or "Asian" - specific cultural contexts included
- ✓ Mixed heritage personas (3) reflect growing mixed-race population

**Gaps**:
- No Bangladeshi representation
- No Eastern European representation
- No Middle Eastern/Arab representation (though discrimination is referenced in WRES for Arab staff)

**Verdict**: Ethnicity representation is **intentionally overweighted** for testing purposes and **appropriately diverse** in heritage specificity. Justified by testing requirements.
